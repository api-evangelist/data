The west catches up with information transparency bandwagon.The Western Australian government has kicked off efforts to catch up with its fellow states when it comes to unlocking the economic and democratic value of public sector data stores.WA, with geospatial agency Landgate at the helm, has aired the first draft of its inaugural open data policy, which entreats on agencies to make proactive data release their default position.Geological information is big business in the state.Already this year the Department of Mining and Petroleum has injected 130 years of exploration data into its digital bedrock geology map, in an effort to attract miners to WA over its competing neighbours.
