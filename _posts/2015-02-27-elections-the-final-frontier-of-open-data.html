It’s hard to imagine that we lack open data in elections when the 24-hour news media does nothing but shower us with data during election season.It gives us the horse race totals of candidate campaign donations during the election run-up, provides minute-to-minute election results on wall sized interactive displays and can cite the lowest voter turnout rates since fill-in-the-blank as is customary in the post-election rumination.But the deluge of information doesn’t necessarily mean election data conforms to the standards of open data.If anything, the question of openness might have been obfuscated by its availability.So, Sunlight will examine these issues in a coming series that looks at elections through the lens of open data and explore the following vital questions: With the Knight News Challenge focusing on elections this year, we hope this series will help shine some light on the state of open elections data.
